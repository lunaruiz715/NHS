What is marble? In what ways do people utilize marble?

Marble is a type of metamorphic rock derived from the recrystallization of sedimentary limestone exposed to immense heat and pressure. This process occurs to limestone deep within the earth and is associated with tectonic activity. Differences in the duration, heat, pressure, and other impurities present during metamorphism can lead to differences in the marble type produced at the structural and chemical level. Marble is primarily composed of calcium carbonate that is both structurally homogenous and fine grained, and usually contains other additional chemical impurities (e.g., metal oxides, silicates). These impurities can lead the naturally pure white marble to gain unique patterning (e.g., veins, branches) and overall color variations across the color spectrum. Marble can be found throughout the globe, is accessed typically through mining and is utilized as both a dimensioned stone and as an aggregate material. As dimensioned stone, marble has been most notably used in the construction architectural design elements. From sculptures and monuments to flooring, walls, countertops, basins, fountains, and even entire buildings, marble has been widely applied. Some famous examples are be the Statue of David (by Michelangelo), the Taj Mahal (Agra, India), the Washington Monument (Washington D.C., USA), and the Pantheon (Rome, Italy). As an aggregate material such as crushed stone chips, marble can be used in construction and landscaping for foundations, rock gardens, roads and pathways due to being resistant to weathering, and found not to harbour pests or allergens. Additionally, when ground into a powder, marble is used within the agricultural sector to neutralize acidic soils (when heated into calcium oxide) and incorporated into animal feed as a nutrient calcium source. Ground marble has also been used as a standard acid neutralizing agent in the chemical industry and healthcare (e.g., antacids), as a material whitener (e.g., paints, paper, pastes), and as a mild abrasive in cleaning or scrubbing products.
